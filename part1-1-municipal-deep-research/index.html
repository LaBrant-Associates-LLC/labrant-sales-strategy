    Part 1.1: Municipal Painting Market Intelligence | SE Wisconsin 100-Mile Radius¶

    Date: 12/22/25 | Revision: #0 Changelog: Initial deep research revision - added 80+ sources, historical bid data, competitive intelligence, decision-maker contacts, fire hydrant verification across 21 utilities

    A non-union painting contractor with DBE (WisDOT), SBE (City of Milwaukee), and Wisconsin DHS lead certifications has significant competitive advantages in this $50M+ annual market. The massive $43 million Milwaukee Public Schools lead remediation project completed in December 2025 demonstrates the scale of opportunity, while upcoming cosmetic painting phases in Spring 2026 present immediate bidding potential.

    Historical Contract Awards (36-Month Analysis)¶

    The past 36 months showed substantial municipal painting activity dominated by a handful of certified contractors. JCP Construction LLC, a Milwaukee-based minority-owned firm, won the landmark MPS lead remediation contract, deploying 865+ workers across 99 schools and 7 million square feet. The original $20 million budget ballooned to $43 million due to scope expansion, with JCP subcontracting to Service Painting Corporation and Independence Painting Company (Des Plaines, IL).

    Certification Advantages and Requirements¶

    City of Milwaukee SBE Program¶

    Bid Preference: 10% (up to $30,000 cap) for certified Local Business Enterprises with SBE status

    Participation Goal: 25% SBE on contracts over $25,000

    SRC Affidavit: 5% preference (up to $25,000) for Socially-Responsible Contractors

    Compliance Tracking: B2Gnow system for contracts over $25,000

    Certification Contact: Office of Equity and Inclusion, (414) 286-5553

    Milwaukee County TBE Program¶

    Construction Goal: 25% Targeted Business Enterprise participation

    Professional Services Goal: 17% TBE participation

    Accepted Certifications: - WisUCP DBE (WisDOT, Milwaukee County, City of Madison, Dane County) - Wisconsin DOA MBE/WBE - Milwaukee County SBE

    Contact: CBDP Office, (414) 278-4747

    WisDOT DBE Program - Critical Update¶

    October 2025 Status: U.S. DOT changed DBE/ACDBE rules requiring recertification of all 1,296 certified firms. WisDOT has temporarily paused setting DBE goals for federally funded contracts until recertification completes.

    Impact: DBE certification remains valid for Milwaukee County TBE recognition (non-federal)

    Contact: osecdbe@dot.wi.gov for recertification status

    Wisconsin DHS Lead Certification¶

    Requirement: Mandatory for pre-1978 building work disturbing more than 6 SF interior or 20 SF exterior painted surfaces

    Certification Fees: - Lead-Safe Renovator: $75-150 (2-4 year validity) - Abatement Supervisor: $275+

    Competitive Advantage: Many painting contractors lack this certification, limiting competition on pre-1978 building work (majority of Milwaukee building stock)

    Contact: DHSAsbestosLead@dhs.wisconsin.gov or (608) 261-6876

    Conclusion¶

    The 100-mile Milwaukee radius contains approximately 150+ municipalities and 11 counties with potential painting contract opportunities. The combination of DBE (WisDOT), SBE (Milwaukee), and DHS lead certification positions a contractor for virtually all municipal painting opportunities in the region, with bid preferences and participation goals creating measurable competitive advantages.

    Fire hydrant painting is NOT a viable municipal opportunity - 20 of 21 surveyed utilities handle this work in-house. Only Oconomowoc contracts out, representing ~700 hydrants every 3 years.

    Priority Targets: 1. City of Milwaukee - Highest certification advantage (10% bid preference + 25% SBE goal) 2. Milwaukee County - TBE recognition (25% construction goal) 3. Milwaukee Public Schools - Phase 2 cosmetic painting ($5-15M estimated) 4. MMSD - 22%+ SWMBE utilization exceeds goals 5. HACM - Section 3 requirements favor local certified firms

    Immediate Actions: 1. Complete all platform registrations by January 15 2. Submit prequalification paperwork by January 31 3. Contact MPS Procurement about Phase 2 timeline 4. Monitor water tower RFPs in January-March 5. Build GC relationships for subcontracting opportunities

    The late December timing means few current opportunities exist, but establishing registrations now positions the company for the spring 2026 bidding season when exterior painting and cosmetic school contracts typically release.
