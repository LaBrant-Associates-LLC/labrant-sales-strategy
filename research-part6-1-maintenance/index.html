    Part 6.1: Recurring Facility Maintenance Painting Contracts | SE Wisconsin¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: #0 Changelog: Initial research - Large venue maintenance, T&M contracts, handrails/steel/infrastructure repainting

    This document identifies recurring maintenance painting opportunities at large public facilitiesdistinct from new construction. These facilities have ongoing maintenance cycles for handrails, steel, fencing, bleachers, signage, and building components that create predictable annual work regardless of capital project status.

    Milwaukee County: Contract 5 Painting Pool¶

    The Opportunity¶

    Milwaukee County maintains Time & Materials (T&M) contract pools for facility maintenance work. Contract 5 specifically covers Painting across County-owned facilities.

    Scope includes: - Courthouse Complex (Historic Courthouse, Criminal Justice Facility, Public Safety Building) - Safety Building - Medical Examiner Building - Marcia P. Coggs Human Services Center - Children's Adolescent Treatment Center - Vel R. Phillips Youth and Family Justice Center - Facilities West - Senior Centers - Wil-O-Way Centers

    Contract Structure¶

    Contract Trade
    Contract 1 General Construction
    Contract 2 Plumbing
    Contract 3 Heating & Ventilating
    Contract 4 Carpet & Resilient Flooring
    Contract 5 Painting
    Contract 6 Metal Fabrication

    Key Contact¶

    Name Title Phone Email
    Tom Travia Project Contact, Facilities Management (414) 278-4770 tom.travia@milwaukeecountywi.gov

    Participation Requirements¶

    • 25% TBE participation goal on Milwaukee County projects
    • Your WisDOT DBE certification provides automatic TBE recognition
    • B2GNow diversity compliance system used for tracking

    Contract Status & Timing¶

    The current contract pool was bid in March 2019 (Project 5702-19404). These T&M contracts typically run 3-5 years with renewal options.

    Action: Contact Tom Travia to inquire about: 1. Current contract status and expiration 2. Upcoming rebid timeline 3. How to get on the approved vendor list for callout work

    Milwaukee County Zoo: 65% TBE Goal¶

    The Opportunity¶

    The Milwaukee County Zoo has a published 65% TBE goal for time and materials contractsthe highest TBE target identified in this research. This applies to routine maintenance work.

    Facility Profile¶

    Metric Data
    Grounds 197 acres
    Buildings requiring HVAC 48
    Additional buildings to maintain 81
    Total structures 129+
    Division Maintenance & Facilities (M&F)

    Painting Opportunities¶

    The Zoo has extensive infrastructure requiring ongoing maintenance painting: - Handrails throughout guest walkways (miles of steel/aluminum railings) - Fencing around animal habitats - Outdoor structures (shade pavilions, restrooms, concession stands) - Signage and wayfinding systems - Exhibit structures (observation decks, boardwalks, bridges) - Building exteriors across 129+ structures - Playground equipment steel components - Utility infrastructure (bollards, light poles, benches)

    Strategic Advantage¶

    At 65% TBE participation, nearly two-thirds of T&M contract value should go to certified firms. Your TBE certification positions you for substantial recurring work.

    Key Contacts¶

    Name Title Source
    Amos Morris Zoo Director (414) 256-5412
    Zoo Facilities Maintenance & Facilities Division Via Zoo Administration

    How to Connect¶

    1. Contact Milwaukee County Zoo Administration
    2. Request meeting with M&F Division regarding maintenance painting vendor list
    3. Emphasize TBE certification and painting capabilities
    4. Inquire about annual T&M contract opportunities

    Facility Maintenance: Scope of Work Examples¶

    Handrail Painting (Common Specification)¶

    Preparation: - Clean surfaces of dirt, grease, and loose paint - Sand or wire brush to remove rust and create profile - Prime bare metal with rust-inhibitive primer

    Application: - 2 coats high-performance exterior enamel - 2.5-3.5 mils DFT per coat - Colors to match existing or owner specification

    Typical Pricing Factors: - Linear footage of railing - Height and accessibility - Condition (light touch-up vs. full repaint) - Containment requirements

    Structural Steel Touch-Up¶

    Preparation: - SSPC-SP2 or SP3 surface preparation - Spot prime bare metal - Feather edges to sound paint

    Application: - Match existing coating system - Industrial enamel or polyurethane topcoat - Document coating system for owner records

    Conclusion¶

    Recurring maintenance painting at large facilities represents a more predictable revenue stream than new construction bidding. The key opportunities identified:

    1. Milwaukee County Zoo (65% TBE): Highest certification leverage for T&M maintenance work. Miles of handrails, 129+ buildings, extensive infrastructure.

    2. Milwaukee County Contract 5 Painting Pool: Direct access to maintenance painting across County facilities. Contact Tom Travia immediately.

    3. American Family Field: 24,000 tons of structural steel requiring periodic repainting. $500M+ maintenance program underway.

    4. State Fair Park: 25% minority goal on 190+ acre facility with extensive painting needs.

    5. Wisconsin Center District: Three major venues with post-expansion maintenance programs being established.

    Priority Action: Contact Tom Travia (414-278-4770) and Milwaukee County Zoo to understand current T&M contract structure and how to participate.
