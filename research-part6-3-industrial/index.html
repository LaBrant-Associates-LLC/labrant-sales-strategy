    Part 6.3: Emerging Industrial, Private Facilities & Niche Markets | SE Wisconsin¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: 0 Changelog: Initial research baseline - Industrial facilities, private clubs, religious institutions, food processing, utilities, transit facilities

    Executive Summary¶

    This supplemental research expands the market intelligence framework beyond traditional municipal and commercial segments into emerging industrial opportunities, private institutional facilities, and niche markets that align with LaBrant & Associates' non-union, certified contractor positioning. Key discoveries include:

    1. I-94 East-West Corridor Project: $1.7 billion infrastructure project with construction starting Fall 2025 - creates massive downstream opportunities
    2. Private Country Clubs: Active renovation cycle with $2.5M+ projects at Blue Mound, Ozaukee CC (2026 clubhouse renovation planned)
    3. Religious Institutions: Archdiocese of Milwaukee's ~200 parishes represent significant ongoing maintenance market
    4. Industrial Corridor: HSA Commercial Real Estate building 1.9 million SF of speculative warehouse space along I-94
    5. Automotive Dealerships: Russ Darrow Group operates 15+ locations with 3 body shops and ongoing facility needs

    Critical Finding: Many of these segments operate outside union-dominated procurement channels, creating favorable competitive dynamics for certified merit-shop contractors.

    Section 1: Major Utility & Industrial Facilities¶

    We Energies / WEC Energy Group¶

    Corporate Profile: - Parent: WEC Energy Group (NYSE: WEC) - Headquarters: Milwaukee, Wisconsin - Service Area: 4.4 million customers across four states - Major Subsidiaries: We Energies, Wisconsin Public Service Corp.

    Active Power Plant Projects:

    Facility Status Investment Notes
    Oak Creek Natural Gas Plant Construction 2025-2028 $1.2 billion 1,100 MW capacity, 5 turbines, replacing retiring coal units
    Paris Generating Station Proposed $280 million Gas-fired reciprocating engines, 130 MW
    LNG Storage Facility Proposed $200 million Oak Creek location
    33-Mile Pipeline Proposed $183 million Connecting Oak Creek to Paris

    Retiring Facilities (Potential Decommissioning Work): - Oak Creek Coal Units 5-8: Retiring 2025-2026 (~$650M remaining book value) - Columbia Generating Station units: Retirement 2029 - Pleasant Prairie Power Plant: Already closed, securitization complete

    Industrial Painting Opportunities: - New construction structural steel coating (gas turbine buildings) - Equipment painting (turbines, generators, piping systems) - Tank coating (LNG storage, fuel systems) - Decommissioning: Protective coatings for retired equipment, demolition prep - Ongoing maintenance: 1,000-acre Oak Creek site

    Access Strategy: - Contact: WEC Procurement (corporate.wec.com/suppliers) - Industrial coatings typically require SSPC/NACE certifications - Safety certifications critical (confined space, fall protection, hot work) - Note: Major utility construction typically uses union contractors, but maintenance/specialty work may have more flexibility

    Milwaukee Metropolitan Sewerage District (MMSD)¶

    Previously covered in Part 6.2 - Key supplement:

    Industrial Coating Specialization Required: - Wastewater treatment environments are highly corrosive - Typical coatings: Epoxy linings, polyurea, coal tar epoxy alternatives - Certifications beneficial: SSPC-QP1 (Shop), SSPC-QP2 (Field) - Confined space entry: Standard requirement for tank/vessel work

    Long-Term Opportunity: Consider pursuing SSPC certification as capability development to access this $200M+ annual capital budget segment.

    Port Milwaukee (Jones Island)¶

    Facility Profile: - Owner: City of Milwaukee (Board of Harbor Commissioners) - Location: Jones Island, Inner Harbor - Infrastructure: 50+ acres, 15 miles of rail track, heavy-lift cranes (440,000 lb capacity) - Annual Tonnage: ~3 million tons

    Painting Scope Opportunities: - Dockwall structures and timber maintenance - Crane and heavy equipment coating - Terminal buildings and warehouses - Rail infrastructure (track-side structures) - Fencing and security infrastructure - Signage and wayfinding

    Procurement: - Contracts posted: port.milwaukee.gov/Contract-Opportunities - Also posted: City of Milwaukee Daily Reporter, DPW bid notices - E-Notify registration: city.milwaukee.gov for real-time notifications - Recent GC awards: Michels Construction (infrastructure)

    City SBE/LBE Advantage: Port Milwaukee follows City of Milwaukee procurement rules - 25% SBE participation goals apply

    Contact: - Port Milwaukee Procurement - 2323 S. Lincoln Memorial Dr., Milwaukee, WI 53207 - (414) 286-3511

    Section 3: Food & Beverage Manufacturing¶

    Molson Coors Milwaukee Brewery ("Miller Valley")¶

    Facility Profile: - Address: 3939 W. Highland Boulevard, Milwaukee - Status: Oldest large-scale operating brewery in the US (founded 1855) - Campus: Multiple buildings including corporate offices, brewery, visitor center - Primary GC: Hunzinger Construction (ongoing relationship since 1997+)

    Recent Projects (Hunzinger GC): - 5-story Buildings 66 and 66A renovations + skywalk - MolsonCoors Health and Fitness Center - Great Lakes Regional Offices (Building 66, 1st floor) - MolsonCoors University (Kahler Slater Architects) - Innovation Center (Zimmerman Design Group)

    Painting Opportunities: - Corporate office renovations - Brewery maintenance (industrial environments) - Visitor Center/tour facilities - Exterior building maintenance - Parking structures

    Access Challenge: Hunzinger is union-signatory GC - subcontracting opportunities limited for non-union contractors. However, direct facilities maintenance relationships may be possible for smaller scopes.

    Alternative Approach: - Contact Molson Coors Facilities Management directly - Focus on maintenance/refresh work outside major construction projects - Building age (1855 origins) means ongoing maintenance requirements

    Klement's Sausage Company¶

    Facility Profile: - Address: 207 E. Lincoln Avenue, Milwaukee (South Side) - Founded: 1956 by Klement brothers - Recent: Acquired by Amylu Foods (2024) - Facilities: Main processing plant, Chase Avenue distribution center (85,000 SF)

    Food Processing Environment Considerations: - FDA/USDA compliance requirements for coatings - Food-safe paint products required in production areas - Washdown-resistant coatings for processing rooms - Stainless steel and epoxy flooring systems

    Opportunity: Ownership transition often triggers facility assessments and upgrades. Contact new ownership (Amylu Foods) regarding facility maintenance plans.

    Section 4: I-94 Corridor Industrial Development¶

    I-94 East-West Freeway Project¶

    Project Overview: - Scope: 3.5 miles from 70th Street to 16th Street - Investment: $1.7 billion - Timeline: West Leg construction 2025-2028, East Leg 2028+, Stadium Interchange 2029+ - GC Selection: Ongoing/TBD

    Downstream Opportunities: This massive infrastructure project creates secondary opportunities: - Adjacent property improvements - Business relocations requiring facility work - Post-construction commercial development - Stadium Interchange area redevelopment

    Recent I-94 North-South Corridor Development: Major developments completed along I-94 corridor: - Amazon warehouse and distribution facility - Uline corporate headquarters expansion - Foxconn campus (scaled back but $600M invested) - General Mills distribution center (323,000 SF) - Multiple HSA Commercial speculative warehouses

    Section 5: Religious Institutions¶

    Archdiocese of Milwaukee¶

    Scope: - Territory: 10 Wisconsin counties (Dodge, Fond du Lac, Kenosha, Milwaukee, Ozaukee, Racine, Sheboygan, Walworth, Washington, Waukesha) - Parishes: ~200 active parishes - Catholic Population: 591,890 registered members (2017) - Total Property Value: ~$900 million (Milwaukee alone)

    Active Major Projects:

    Project Location Scope GC Budget
    Gesu Church Renovation 1145 W. Wisconsin Ave Interior restoration, pews, painting, lighting, AV CG Schmidt $10M+
    Marquette University Chapel Campus Multi-faith renovation, electrochromic glass CG Schmidt Significant

    Gesu Church Project Details (Urban Spelunking coverage): - Scope: New flooring, new pews, ceiling/wall painting, lighting upgrade, AV system, baptismal font, accessibility ramps - Woodwork: Inspired Artisans (Tassara family, Milwaukee) - Historic: Henry C. Koch design (1894), National Register - Timeline: 2025 completion - Note: CG Schmidt is union-signatory - limited direct opportunity, but demonstrates active parish renovation market

    Parish Maintenance Market: Beyond major renovations, 200 parishes represent ongoing maintenance opportunities: - Interior repainting cycles (typically 7-10 years) - Rectory and school maintenance - Parish hall and meeting space refreshes - Exterior maintenance (historic buildings)

    Access Strategy: 1. Contact Archdiocese Facilities/Property Management office 2. Build relationships with individual parish facility managers 3. Emphasize historic building experience and lead certification 4. Many parishes use local contractors for maintenance vs. major GCs for construction

    Archdiocese Contact: - Chancery Office: 3501 S. Lake Drive, Milwaukee, WI 53207 - Phone: (414) 769-3341 - Website: archmil.org

    Country Club Access Strategy¶

    Key Differentiators: - Private clubs value discretion and member experience - Work typically scheduled around golf/event calendar - Off-season (November-March) preferred for major work - Quality over price often prioritized - Long-term maintenance relationships valued

    Approach: 1. Contact Club General Manager or Facilities Director 2. Request vendor qualification information 3. Emphasize: Minimal member disruption, quality workmanship, schedule flexibility 4. Build relationships through smaller maintenance projects first 5. Reference experience with similar private facilities

    Timing: - Major renovations: Plan in winter months - Exterior work: Spring/fall (avoiding peak golf season) - Interior clubhouse: November-March optimal
