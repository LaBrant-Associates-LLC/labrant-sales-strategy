    I-94 Corridor Industrial Development: SE Wisconsin Market Intelligence¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: 0 Segment Value: MASSIVE - $25B+ development corridor, speculative construction, tenant improvement opportunities

    Executive Summary¶

    The I-94 corridor between Milwaukee and Chicago represents one of the hottest industrial development markets in the Midwest:

    • $1.7 billion I-94 East-West freeway project (2025-2029)
    • 1.9+ million SF of speculative warehouse construction by HSA Commercial alone
    • Major corporate investments: Amazon, Uline, Foxconn, Milwaukee Tool
    • Tenant improvement painting opportunities as spec buildings lease up
    • New construction painting for ongoing development
    • Property management relationships for ongoing maintenance

    Strategic Opportunity: Position as preferred painting contractor for industrial developers and property managers along the corridor.

    Market Overview¶

    Geographic Scope¶

    I-94 Corridor Definition: - North: Milwaukee (downtown) - South: Illinois state line (Kenosha County) - East/West: ~10 miles either side of I-94

    Key Development Zones:

    Zone Counties Major Activity
    Milwaukee Metro Milwaukee Urban infill, I-94 East-West
    Racine Gateway Racine Foxconn area, distribution
    Kenosha Industrial Kenosha Amazon, spec warehouses
    Bristol/Pleasant Prairie Kenosha HSA projects, new development

    Why I-94 Corridor Is Hot¶

    Factor Impact
    Chicago-Milwaukee link 9.5M + 1.6M population access
    Infrastructure investment $1.7B I-94 East-West project
    Business climate WI vs. IL tax advantages
    Labor availability Cross-state workforce
    Land costs Lower than Chicago suburbs
    Distribution efficiency Central US location

    Speculative Building Inventory¶

    What Is Spec Development?¶

    Developers build warehouses without tenants committed, betting on market demand. This creates:

    1. Initial painting during construction
    2. Tenant improvement painting when leases signed
    3. Ongoing maintenance after occupancy

    Current Spec Pipeline (Estimated)¶

    Status Square Feet Opportunity Phase
    Under construction 2-3 million SF New construction now
    Recently completed (vacant) 1-2 million SF TI painting imminent
    Planned/announced 3-5 million SF Future pipeline

    When Spec Buildings Lease:

    TI Component Painting Scope
    Office build-out Walls, ceilings, trim
    Warehouse demising Separation walls
    Loading dock areas Safety striping, walls
    Break rooms Full interior
    Restrooms Specialty coatings
    Exterior signage areas Accent painting

    Typical TI Painting Budget: - Small tenant (50,000 SF): $15,000-$30,000 - Medium tenant (150,000 SF): $40,000-$80,000 - Large tenant (300,000+ SF): $75,000-$150,000+

    Property Management Relationships¶

    Why Property Managers Matter¶

    Industrial buildings are often owned by: - REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) - Private equity funds - Institutional investors

    These owners use third-party property managers for maintenance, creating: - Vendor qualification opportunities - Multiple building relationships - Recurring maintenance contracts

    Major Industrial Property Managers¶

    Company Focus Buildings
    CBRE Full service Multiple I-94 properties
    JLL Full service Industrial portfolio
    Cushman & Wakefield Full service Regional presence
    Colliers Full service Milwaukee office
    NAI MLG Commercial Local SE Wisconsin focus
    HSA Commercial (self-managed) Developer-owned HSA properties

    Building Vendor Qualification¶

    Typical Requirements: - Insurance certificates (GL, Auto, WC) - W-9 / tax documentation - References (3-5 similar projects) - Safety program documentation - Financial stability evidence

    Process: 1. Contact property manager 2. Request vendor application 3. Complete qualification package 4. Get added to approved vendor list 5. Receive bid opportunities

    Industrial Painting Specifications¶

    Warehouse Interior Standards¶

    Area Coating Type Notes
    Walls (CMU) Block filler + latex White/light gray typical
    Walls (metal) DTM (direct to metal) Prep critical
    Steel structure Industrial enamel Touch-up common
    Dock doors Industrial enamel High durability
    Office areas Commercial latex Standard commercial

    Floor Coatings¶

    Area System Notes
    Warehouse floor Typically concrete sealer Full coating rare
    Dock areas Epoxy optional High traffic zones
    Office areas Standard finishes Carpet/tile typical
    Restrooms Epoxy or tile Moisture resistance

    Safety Striping¶

    Marking Color Purpose
    Pedestrian paths Yellow Walking areas
    Forklift zones Yellow/black Traffic separation
    Fire equipment Red Extinguisher locations
    Emergency exits Green Egress paths
    Dock positions White Trailer alignment

    Outreach Approach¶

    To Developers (HSA, Scannell, etc.):

    "We're a commercial painting contractor focused on industrial facilities along the I-94 corridor. We'd like to introduce our services for your upcoming projects in Kenosha and Racine counties.

    We offer competitive pricing, strong safety programs, and the capacity to scale for large warehouse projects. May I learn more about your subcontractor qualification process?"

    To Property Managers:

    "We're reaching out to introduce our commercial painting services for your industrial portfolio in southeastern Wisconsin.

    We specialize in tenant improvement painting, warehouse maintenance, and safety striping. I'd like to get qualified as an approved vendor for your properties.

    Could you direct me to your vendor qualification process?"
