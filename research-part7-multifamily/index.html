    Part 7: Wisconsin Multifamily Operators & Development Pipeline | SE Wisconsin Focus¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: 1 Changelog: Rev 1 - Expanded from Rev 0 with extensive development pipeline research, hotel projects, religious institution renovations, retail developments (Scheels/Mayfair), merit-shop GC intelligence (Stevens, VJS, Greenfire), and additional operator contacts

    Executive Summary¶

    Wisconsin's multifamily market operates through regional relationships rather than national vendor procurement systems. Key finding: Most major national REITs (AvalonBay, Equity Residential, MAA, UDR, Camden, Essex) have NO Wisconsin presence—they focus on coastal and Sun Belt markets. This creates opportunity for local certification advantages.

    2025 Milwaukee Market Outlook: - Occupancy rate: 95.9% (6th nationally among major markets) - Average rent: ~$1,439/month projected Q4 2025 - New construction declining 40% vs 2024 (lowest since 2015) - Rent growth: 2.9-3.2% projected 2025 - Strongest submarkets: Outlying Waukesha County (+4.1%), Southern Ozaukee County (+4.0%)

    KEY STRATEGIC FINDINGS¶

    1. Wisconsin's multifamily market is relationship-based, not procurement-system-based. Local certifications and personal connections matter more than national vendor portals.

    2. AOMA membership provides direct access to operators controlling 50,000+ units through single networking investment.

    3. Merit-shop GCs dominate the opportunity set: Stevens, Greenfire, VJS have active pipelines in target markets.

    4. 100 East Tower ($165M conversion) represents a major near-term opportunity—Stevens Construction confirmed as co-GC.

    5. Capri Communities' vertically integrated model (Tarantino & Company development arm) creates ongoing pipeline across 43 properties.

    6. Historic conversions favor DHS lead certification—J. Jeffers, Klein Development, 100 East all involve pre-1978 buildings.

    7. Senior living market strength: Capri, VJS, Greenfire all have active senior living portfolios—aligns perfectly with LaBrant target markets.
