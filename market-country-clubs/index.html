    Private Country Clubs: SE Wisconsin Market Intelligence¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: 0 Segment Value: HIGH - Active renovation cycle, merit-shop friendly, relationship-driven

    Executive Summary¶

    Southeast Wisconsin's private country club market represents a $10M+ annual renovation opportunity with favorable dynamics for certified merit-shop contractors:

    • 20+ private clubs in Milwaukee metro area
    • Active renovation cycle driven by membership competition
    • $1-3M typical clubhouse renovation budgets
    • Decentralized procurement (club boards, not public bid process)
    • Relationship-driven vendor selection (quality over lowest price)
    • Off-season scheduling aligns with painting contractor capacity

    Immediate Opportunity: Ozaukee Country Club's 2026 clubhouse renovation is in planning now - contact immediately to get on bid list.

    Market Dynamics¶

    Why Clubs Are Investing Now¶

    1. Membership Competition: Clubs compete for younger members (30s-40s demographics)
    2. Casualization Trend: Outdoor patios, relaxed dress codes driving renovations
    3. Deferred Maintenance: Many clubhouses 50-100+ years old
    4. Post-COVID Recovery: Events/weddings revenue returning, funding capital projects
    5. Real Estate Values: Club properties are valuable assets worth maintaining

    Renovation Cycle Patterns¶

    Renovation Type Typical Cycle Budget Range
    Interior repaint 7-10 years $50K-$150K
    Clubhouse renovation 15-25 years $1M-$5M
    Locker room refresh 10-15 years $200K-$500K
    Pool/aquatics 15-20 years $500K-$2M
    Pro shop/entry 10-15 years $100K-$300K

    Competitive Landscape¶

    Who's Painting These Clubs?¶

    Private clubs typically use: 1. Local painting contractors with existing relationships 2. GC subcontractors for major renovations 3. In-house maintenance for touch-up work

    Key Insight: Clubs value relationship continuity - once you're in, you tend to stay in for ongoing work.

    Differentiation Strategy¶

    To win club business, emphasize:

    Factor Why It Matters
    Minimal Disruption Members expect uninterrupted experience
    Schedule Flexibility Work around events, golf seasons
    Quality Over Price Clubs will pay more for better results
    Discretion Member privacy is paramount
    Historic Sensitivity Many clubs have historic features
    References Other club experience is gold

    Sales Approach¶

    Initial Contact Script¶

    Phone/Email to General Manager:

    "Good morning, I'm [Name] with LaBrant & Associates, a commercial painting contractor serving southeastern Wisconsin. We specialize in private club and hospitality facilities, and I noticed [Club Name] has been making some wonderful improvements to your facilities.

    I'd like to introduce our company as a potential resource for your painting and coating needs - whether for upcoming renovations or ongoing maintenance. We understand the importance of minimal member disruption and scheduling work during your off-season.

    Would you have 15 minutes to discuss your facility's painting needs, or could you direct me to the appropriate person on your facilities team?"

    Capability Statement Points¶

    For club-specific capability statements, emphasize:

    1. Club/Hospitality Experience (or comparable high-end commercial)
    2. Off-Season Scheduling Capability
    3. Phased Work Approach (minimize disruption)
    4. Quality Workmanship (not lowest bidder positioning)
    5. Insurance & Safety (appropriate coverage levels)
    6. Local References (other clubs or comparable facilities)
    7. Historic Building Experience (many clubs are 75-100+ years old)

    Objection Handling¶

    Objection Response
    "We have a painter" "We'd welcome the opportunity to be a secondary resource or quote your next larger project"
    "Board decides" "Would you be willing to share our information with the board, or let me know when they review vendors?"
    "Not in budget this year" "When do you typically plan your capital projects? I'd like to stay in touch for future needs"
    "We use our GC's painter" "For major renovations, that makes sense. What about maintenance and refresh work between projects?"

    Pricing Considerations¶

    Club Pricing vs. Standard Commercial¶

    Private clubs typically expect: - Premium pricing (10-20% above standard commercial) - Quality-focused proposals (not lowest bid) - Detailed scope (clubs are detail-oriented) - Flexible scheduling (built into pricing) - Professional presentation (proposals reflect on contractor)

    Scope Typical Range Notes

    Pro shop $5,000-$15,000 Retail environment
    Exterior trim $15,000-$50,000 Historic details, access
    Pool house $8,000-$20,000 Moisture-resistant coatings

    Key Success Factors¶

    1. Act Fast on Ozaukee - 2026 renovation is imminent
    2. Build Relationships First - Don't lead with "we want your business"
    3. Demonstrate Quality - Clubs want best work, not cheapest
    4. Schedule Flexibility - Off-season availability is critical
    5. Discretion - Never discuss club business publicly
    6. References - One club reference opens doors to others
    7. Patience - Club relationships take time to develop
