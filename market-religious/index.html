    Religious Institutions: Archdiocese of Milwaukee Market Intelligence¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: 0 Segment Value: HIGH - 200 parishes, $900M property value, decentralized procurement, lead cert advantage

    Executive Summary¶

    The Archdiocese of Milwaukee represents one of the largest untapped markets for commercial painting in southeastern Wisconsin:

    • ~200 active parishes across 10 counties
    • $900+ million in property value (Milwaukee alone)
    • 591,890 registered Catholics (2017)
    • Historic buildings requiring specialized care (many pre-1978 = lead certification required)
    • Decentralized procurement - individual parishes control vendor selection
    • Ongoing maintenance cycle - churches never stop needing painting

    Critical Advantage: Wisconsin DHS Lead Certification provides competitive edge for pre-1978 buildings (majority of Catholic churches).

    Each parish typically includes: - Church building (sanctuary, nave, vestibule) - Rectory (priest residence) - Parish hall/gymnasium - School building (if applicable - ~85 elementary schools) - Convent (if applicable - increasingly repurposed) - Administrative offices - Parking structures/lots

    Annual Market Size Estimate¶

    Conservative Calculation: - 200 parishes × average $15,000/year maintenance = $3M annual market - Plus major renovations (5-10 parishes/year × $50,000 average) = $250K-$500K - Total Addressable Market: $3-4M annually

    Lead Certification Advantage¶

    Why This Matters¶

    Building Age Distribution (Estimate): - Pre-1900: ~20% of churches (historic downtown parishes) - 1900-1950: ~40% of churches (immigrant-era construction) - 1950-1978: ~30% of churches (suburban expansion) - Post-1978: ~10% of churches (recent construction)

    Result: ~90% of Catholic churches may contain lead paint

    Wisconsin DHS Lead Certification Requirements¶

    For renovation/painting work on pre-1978 buildings: - Certified renovator required - Lead-safe work practices mandatory - Proper containment and cleanup - Documentation requirements

    LaBrant Advantage: DHS Lead Certification creates barrier to entry for uncertified competitors.

    Marketing the Lead Advantage¶

    To Parish Administrators:

    "As a Wisconsin DHS certified lead contractor, we ensure your renovation project complies with EPA and state regulations while protecting parishioners, students, and staff from lead exposure risks. This is especially important for your historic buildings."

    Procurement Process¶

    How Parishes Select Contractors¶

    Typical Process: 1. Facilities Committee identifies need 2. Pastor approves budget allocation 3. Parish Administrator solicits quotes (usually 2-3) 4. Committee reviews and recommends 5. Pastor/Finance Council approves 6. Work scheduled around parish calendar

    Key Decision Makers: | Role | Influence | Contact Priority | |------|-----------|------------------| | Pastor | Final approval | Medium (busy, delegate to admin) | | Parish Administrator | Day-to-day decisions | HIGH | | Business Manager | Budget control | HIGH | | Facilities Committee Chair | Recommendations | Medium | | Maintenance Staff | Vendor relationships | Medium |

    Winning Parish Business¶

    What Parishes Value: 1. Trust/Reliability - Will you show up when promised? 2. Fair Pricing - Not lowest, but reasonable 3. Respect for Sacred Space - Understand church environment 4. Flexibility - Work around Mass schedule 5. References - Other parish experience 6. Lead Certification - Compliance for older buildings 7. Insurance - Appropriate coverage

    Best Approach: - Monday-Friday work (avoid weekend Masses) - Early morning start (complete before evening events) - Phased approach (section by section) - Coordinate with parish schedule

    Pricing Strategy¶

    Parish Pricing Considerations¶

    Factors: - Parishes are non-profit (budget-conscious) - But value quality (sacred space) - Often compare 2-3 quotes - May have deferred maintenance (larger scope) - Historic buildings require more prep

    Pricing Approach: - Competitive but not lowest - Emphasize value (lead certification, quality) - Offer phased options for budget constraints - Include detailed scope (parishes appreciate transparency)
