    Part 8: New Market Development | SE Wisconsin¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: #0 Changelog: Initial research on untapped markets, new GCs, and specific projects LaBrant is not currently pursuing

    Major Retail: Scheels at Mayfair Mall¶

    Scheels is renovating the 210,000 SF former Boston Store at Mayfair Mall in Wauwatosa. Construction starts 2026, opening Spring 2027. This is the largest retail construction project in the Milwaukee metro.

    Project Details: - Location: Mayfair Mall, Wauwatosa (former Boston Store) - Size: 210,000 SF (nearly double Scheels' Appleton/Eau Claire stores) - Value: Full renovation of vacant space, estimated $30-50M construction - Owner: Brookfield Properties (mall owner), Scheels (tenant) - Timeline: Design Review Board approved late 2025, construction 2026, open Spring 2027 - Estimated Painting Scope: $400K-$800K (retail destination with 84 specialty shops, cafe, arcade, candy shop)

    Contact: - City of Wauwatosa Development: Mark Hammond, Development Director - Brookfield Properties: Jonathan Kramer, President Retail Business

    Action: Monitor Daily Reporter for GC announcement. Scheels typically uses regional GCs on their stores. Contact Wauwatosa building department for permit applicant info when filed.

    Major Retail: Mayfair South Apartments¶

    Barrett Lo Visionary Development is building ~900 apartments in four towers at the south end of Mayfair Mall over 10 years. $400 million project with ground-floor retail.

    Project Details: - Developer: Barrett Lo Visionary Development (Milwaukee) - Location: South end of Mayfair Mall site - Units: ~900 across four towers (multi-phase over 10+ years) - Value: $400 million total - TIF: $58 million approved September 2023 - Timeline: Phased construction over next decade

    Contact: Barrett Lo Visionary Development

    Estimated Painting per Phase: $150K-$300K per tower

    Religious Institutions¶

    St. Hedwig Catholic Church (Milwaukee)¶

    Active renovation expected to complete Christmas 2025. If painting is not yet awarded, there may be punch list or Phase 2 work.

    Contact: St. Hedwig Parish Office, sainthedwig.org

    Archdiocese of Milwaukee Capital Campaigns¶

    New Archbishop Jeffrey S. Grob installed November 2024. New leadership often triggers capital campaigns and deferred maintenance projects across the 10-county archdiocese (94 elementary schools, 13 high schools, 5 colleges/universities, 200+ parishes).

    Contact: Archdiocese of Milwaukee Facilities Office, (414) 769-3300

    Catalyst Construction Church Portfolio¶

    Catalyst is a merit-shop GC specializing in religious and private school construction. Recent church projects include: - Evolve Church (Milwaukee): 23,000 SF indoor park/play area, completed 2022 - Lakewood Baptist/Lakeland Community Church (Pewaukee): 18,898 SF worship addition - Lakeland Community Church: 30,000 SF addition with worship, gathering, office space - St. Peter Catholic Church (Slinger): Major renovation and sanctuary expansion - Southbrook Church: Multi-phase expansion - Christ Church (Mequon): 35,000 SF addition, Children's Ministry renovation - St. Peter Lutheran Church and School: Gymnasium, classrooms, locker rooms, narthex

    Contact: Catalyst Construction, 414-727-6840, catalystbuilds.com

    Action: Contact Catalyst to get on their subcontractor bid list for church and private school projects.

    Private Schools¶

    Augustine Preparatory Academy (Aug Prep) - North Side Campus¶

    $104 million expansion at former Cardinal Stritch University campus. Construction underway, opening August 2026.

    Project Details: - Location: Former Cardinal Stritch University campus, north side Milwaukee - Value: $104 million (100% philanthropy - Ramirez family/Husco International) - Scope: Rebuilding and expanding two buildings into high school + elementary/middle school - Capacity: 330 students initially (K4-6th grade, 9th grade), expanding to full K-12 - Timeline: Work done by May 2026, open August 2026 - GC: Unknown - research needed

    Contact: Aug Prep, Matt Miller (President), Abby Andrietsch (CEO)

    Action: Research GC on this project. $104M construction value suggests $1.5-3M painting scope.

    Untapped Market Segments¶

    Self-Storage Facilities¶

    Growing segment with regular construction. Climate-controlled facilities need interior painting.

    Recent Milwaukee Activity: - Wauwatosa Self Storage: 90,284 SF conversion at 5151 W. State St., 444 units - Developer: Midland Commercial Development Corp (Steve Rolfe), Luft Consulting (William Wirth), Nelson Development (Jordan Nelson) - Architect: Thrive Architects - Opening: Summer 2025 - One Stop Self Storage: 3742 W Wisconsin Ave (opened)

    Typical Scope: Hallways, unit doors, trim, office areas. $25K-$75K per facility.

    Automotive Dealerships¶

    Manufacturers require brand compliance upgrades every few years. Service expansions and EV infrastructure driving renovation activity.

    Major SE Wisconsin Dealer Groups: - Boucher Automotive Group: Multiple brands across SE Wisconsin - Russ Darrow Group: Multiple locations - Schlossmann Auto Group: Subaru, Honda - Miller Motor Sales (Burlington): Ford, Chrysler, Dodge, Jeep, Ram

    Approach: Contact dealer principals or facilities managers directly. Dealerships often bypass traditional GC bidding.

    Typical Scope: Service area painting, showroom refresh, exterior. $15K-$100K per location.

    Property Management Refresh Contracts¶

    Annual turnover painting creates recurring revenue. Heavy May-August for student housing.

    Target Companies: - Bieck Management: Large rental portfolio - Welcome Home Milwaukee: Scattered-site investment properties - Trike Property Management: Large multi-unit buildings - PMI of Greater Milwaukee: National brand, local presence - Performance Asset Management: SE Wisconsin focus

    Approach: Offer annual contracts with fixed unit pricing.

    Light Industrial / Warehouse¶

    I-94 corridor seeing significant development. Target owner-occupied facilities where owners select contractors directly.

    Recent Development: - HSA Commercial: 1.9M SF across four warehouses (Kenosha, Bristol) - Park 94 (Mt. Pleasant): 184-acre Class A industrial park

    Target: 50,000-200,000 SF owner-occupied facilities, not institutional spec buildings.
