    Water Tower Inventory & Coating Market Analysis¶

    Southeastern Wisconsin (100-Mile Radius from Milwaukee)¶

    Date: December 24, 2025 | Revision: 0 Purpose: Market sizing for water tower coating/painting opportunities

    Executive Summary¶

    Water tower coating rehabilitation represents a $150,000-$400,000 per project opportunity with 15-25 year recoating cycles. Research across SE Wisconsin municipalities identified 40+ elevated storage tanks requiring periodic maintenance, with total regional market opportunity estimated at $2-4 million annually based on staggered maintenance schedules.

    Regulatory Framework (Wisconsin NR 811)¶

    Construction Standards¶

    • Materials: Steel, fiberglass, or concrete only (no porous materials)
    • AWWA Standards Required: D100, D102, D103, D104, D110, D115, D120, D130

    Inspection Requirements (NR 810.14)¶

    • Frequency: Professional inspection every 10 years for tanks 10,000+ gallons
    • Scope: Structural integrity, coating condition, safety features

    Coating System Standards¶

    Location Surface Prep Coating System
    Exterior SSPC-SP 6 (Commercial) 3-coat epoxy-urethane
    Wet Interior SSPC-SP 10 (Near White) 3-coat zinc-epoxy
    Dry Interior SSPC-SP 6 (Commercial) 2-coat epoxy

    Safety Requirements¶

    • Ladder cages required
    • Railings and platforms mandatory
    • Guardrails at all access points
    • Locks, fences recommended
    • Intrusion alarms recommended

    Tower Types & Painting Considerations¶

    Composite Elevated Tanks (CET)¶

    • Structure: Concrete pedestal + steel tank
    • Painting Scope: Tank only (concrete unpainted)
    • Advantage: Lower maintenance costs
    • Capacity Range: 500,000 - 3,000,000 gallons
    • Examples: Waukesha Composite Tower (1M gal)

    Traditional Elevated (Full Steel)¶

    • Structure: Steel legs/pedestal + steel tank
    • Painting Scope: Complete structure
    • Higher Maintenance: Full steel requires more coating area
    • Examples: Waukesha Hunter Tower (400K gal)

    Standpipe¶

    • Structure: Tall cylinder, no elevation
    • Typical Size: Low capacity relative to height
    • Example: 72' height 47' diameter = 1M gal

    Spheroid¶

    • Structure: Spherical/elliptical tank on pedestal
    • Typical Capacity: 200,000 - 500,000 gallons

    Conclusion¶

    The SE Wisconsin water tower market represents a $2-4 million annual opportunity with 40+ towers requiring periodic recoating. However, this is a specialized market dominated by Michigan-based contractors with decades of experience and competitive pricing.

    Barriers to Entry: - Significant equipment investment required - SSPC QP1/QP2 certifications needed - Experienced competitors with tight pricing ($215K-$220K spread in recent bids) - Seasonal work concentration (April-October)

    Opportunity Assessment: - High Value: Individual contracts $150K-$500K+ - High Barrier: Specialized equipment, certifications, competition - Recommendation: Consider subcontracting to established tower contractors initially, or focus on related industrial coating work (tanks, silos) to build experience before targeting water towers directly

    Alternative Strategy: Focus on the 10 recurring contract types identified in Part 5 research (property management, religious institutions, dealerships, etc.) which offer lower barriers to entry and align better with current capabilities and certifications.
