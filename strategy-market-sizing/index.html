    Purpose: Planning-level market $ sizing and a segment map to guide 12‑month diversification + 5‑year growth in Wisconsin (no IL/MN).
    Notes: No images included. Dollar figures are ranges and should be refined with your bid log + win rates.

    1A) Wisconsin construction “macro” baseline (source-backed anchors)¶

    Construction value and major drivers (statewide)¶

    • Total value of construction in Wisconsin (2024): ≈ $19.2B (ConstructConnect, via WI Construction Impact Report).
    • That 2024 growth was described as being driven by a large jump in non-residential, with office (including data centers) a major contributor to the year-over-year increase.
    • Residential permits (2024): 23,727 units, up year-over-year; ~54% single-family / ~46% multifamily (units basis).

    Nonresidential spending (statewide)¶

    • Private nonresidential spending in Wisconsin (2024): ≈ $7B
    • State & local spending (2024): ≈ $7B
      (AGC WI fact sheet; totals not available there for residential and certain other categories.)

    1B) Market sizing model for commercial painting/coatings in WI (TAM → SAM)¶

    What we mean by “market” here¶

    For strategy decisions, the most useful view is $ that owners/GCs spend on painting + industrial protective coatings in: - Multifamily (new + turns + exteriors)
    - Private nonresidential (new + TI + repositioning)
    - Industrial maintenance / asset protection (plants, equipment, tanks, structural steel)
    - Select municipal/county work only where union / prevailing-wage friction is manageable

    We exclude: DIY/homeowner retail painting, and we treat heavy union-heavy civil as low-addressability.

    A practical two-bucket sizing approach¶

    Because publicly available datasets don’t cleanly separate “painting on new builds” vs “repaint/maintenance,” we size in two parts:

    Bucket 1 — NEW construction + major renovation painting (easier to anchor)¶

    Private nonresidential new/major reno paint scope (WI): - Anchor: $7B private nonres spend (2024).
    - Typical paint scope on commercial building projects (planning range): ~1.5%–2.5% of total project value.
    - Implied annual paint scope: $105M–$175M (private nonres only).

    Multifamily new paint scope (WI) using permits as a proxy: - Anchor: 23,727 total residential permits in 2024; ~46% multifamily ⇒ ~10,900 multifamily units.
    - Interior paint-only planning range: $2,500–$5,000 per unit ⇒ $27M–$55M.
    - Add common areas + exterior complexity (planning uplift +20%–40%) ⇒ ≈ $32M–$77M.

    Planning take: Bucket 1 (new + major reno) for private nonres + multifamily is ~$140M–$250M+ annually in WI (order-of-magnitude).

    Bucket 2 — REPAINT + maintenance + industrial asset coatings (harder to anchor, but critical)¶

    Repaint/maintenance is often at least comparable to new-paint volume in mature markets because: - interiors cycle every ~5–7 years (units/common areas)
    - exteriors cycle ~7–12 years (weather + substrate)
    - industrial assets (steel, equipment, tanks) have ongoing corrosion-control needs

    Planning range: Bucket 2 is often ~1.0× to 2.5× Bucket 1 for commercial/multifamily/industrial scopes combined (varies with interest rates, new-build cycle, and facility capex).

    Implied repaint/maintenance market (WI): ≈ $140M–$625M.

    Total addressable “commercial + multifamily + industrial coatings” spend (WI)¶

    Planning TAM (statewide): ≈ $280M – $875M per year
    - Lower end = conservative repaint factor + conservative paint scope %
    - Upper end = more repaint-heavy / maintenance-heavy environment

    SAM filters for your business reality (non-union + private-first)¶

    To convert TAM → SAM (serviceable addressable market), discount for: - projects where union-only rules or union GC requirements block entry
    - prevailing wage / public procurement friction
    - scopes packaged with drywall where pricing is structurally compressed (unless you can sell direct or differentiate on schedule/quality)

    A reasonable planning assumption for SAM = ~40%–70% of TAM (statewide), depending on how aggressively you pursue private owners + repeat maintenance. - SAM planning range (WI): ≈ $110M – $610M/year

    1C) Segment map with planning $ ranges (statewide WI)¶

    Important: These ranges are slices of the TAM above, not additive “new money.” Use them to prioritize segments by (1) size, (2) union friction, (3) margin, (4) repeatability.

    Segment What you sell Union friction (planning) Planning $ size (WI) Why it matters
    Multifamily – turns + repositioning unit turns, corridor/common repaint, exterior repaints Low–Medium $80M–$200M Relationship + schedule-driven; less commoditized than new-build low-bid if you win property mgmt trust
    Multifamily – new build full interior/exterior painting Medium $30M–$80M Still your foundation; protect share while competitors “buy volume”
    Private industrial / manufacturing maintenance corrosion-control, equipment/steel, shutdown work Low–Medium $60M–$200M Higher barrier (safety, coatings know-how). Less drywall-bundling pressure
    Warehouses / logistics / self-storage fast-build interiors, metal building exteriors, maintenance Low $30M–$120M Private owners; speed/coverage matters; repeat portfolios possible
    Hospitality (hotels) exterior repaints, room refresh cycles, common areas Low–Medium $20M–$90M Fits your “big exterior repaint” credibility (Hyatt example)
    Retail + tenant improvements (TI) quick-turn interiors, rollouts, brand refresh Low $20M–$80M Great filler work; can build repeat accounts with regional operators
    Private healthcare + senior living occupied-space repainting, infection-control compliant practices Medium $30M–$120M Can be sticky work; qualification-driven; some systems lean union—target independents
    Municipal/county (select) buildings, tanks, small facilities High variance $20M–$100M (but low capture%) Opportunistic only where procurement/prevailing wage doesn’t create constant friction
    Traffic markings (optional expansion) striping + lot markings Medium–High $20M–$80M Often public-facing and price-driven; can be worthwhile if you target private lots/warehouses and bundle with repaint scopes

    1D) “Smart” implications (what these numbers mean for your next decisions)¶

    12-month (near-term) diversification thesis¶

    1. Stop treating “commercial painting” as one market.
      Your real moat is in segments where repeat maintenance + coatings competence matter more than low-bid volume.
    2. Shift the pipeline mix so multifamily is the base, not the whole pie.
      A practical target: multifamily = 60–70% of revenue (still foundation), and 30–40% from 2–3 add-on verticals.
    3. Pick “add-on verticals” that (a) are private-first, and (b) don’t hand advantage to drywall-packagers:
    4. industrial maintenance/coatings
    5. hospitality exteriors + refresh cycles
    6. warehouse/logistics/self-storage portfolios

    5-year (scale) thesis¶

    • Win portfolios, not projects in 2–3 segments: property mgmt groups, regional hotel owners, industrial operators, logistics developers.
    • Build one specialty capability that competitors won’t chase when they’re buying volume (e.g., corrosion-control + complex coatings + safety program depth).

    Appendix A — Sensitivity checks (how to sanity-check the sizing quickly)¶

    Use these 3 knobs to see if the estimate is directionally right for your reality: 1. Private nonres paint % of spend: 1.5% vs 2.5%
    2. Multifamily $/unit paint: $2.5k vs $5k, plus 20–40% for common/exterior
    3. Repaint factor: repaint/maintenance = 1.0× vs 2.5× new-build paint scope

    If your internal last-24-month closed revenue + competitor quotes suggest different unit economics, recalibrate these knobs and the ranges update immediately.

    • “2025 Wisconsin Construction Impact Report” (Leeds School of Business / University of Colorado Boulder; ConstructConnect data references; published Mar 2025) """
