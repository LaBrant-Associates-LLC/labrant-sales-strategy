    Private & Parochial Schools: SE Wisconsin Market Intelligence¶

    Date: 12/25/25 | Revision: 0 Segment Value: HIGH - 100+ schools, summer scheduling window, lead cert critical, repeat business

    Executive Summary¶

    Southeast Wisconsin's private school market offers $2-3M+ annual painting opportunity with characteristics perfectly aligned with LaBrant's capabilities:

    • 100+ private/parochial schools in 10-county area
    • Summer scheduling window (June-August) matches painting contractor capacity
    • Lead certification REQUIRED for most buildings (pre-1978 construction)
    • Decentralized procurement - principals/facility managers decide
    • Repeat business - 5-7 year classroom refresh cycles
    • NON-UNION environment - private schools don't require union labor

    Critical Timing: Summer 2026 school painting must be bid by March-April 2026. Start outreach NOW.

    Winning School Business¶

    Key Success Factors:

    1. Summer Availability - Can you deliver in 10-week window?
    2. Lead Certification - Required for older buildings
    3. References - Other school experience
    4. Crew Size - Can scale up for summer rush?
    5. Quality - Schools inspect carefully before fall
    6. Schedule Reliability - MUST finish before school starts
    7. Background Checks - May require for workers

    Lead Certification Requirements¶

    Regulatory Framework¶

    EPA RRP Rule + Wisconsin DHS: - Applies to pre-1978 buildings - Schools are "child-occupied facilities" (stricter requirements) - Certified renovator must be on-site - Lead-safe work practices mandatory - Documentation and recordkeeping required

    School Building Age Analysis¶

    Era % of Schools Lead Risk Certification Need
    Pre-1950 ~30% HIGH REQUIRED
    1950-1978 ~40% MODERATE REQUIRED
    Post-1978 ~30% LOW Not required

    Result: ~70% of private school buildings require lead-certified contractors

    Marketing Lead Certification¶

    To School Administrators:

    "Federal EPA regulations require certified contractors for any renovation work in schools built before 1978. As a Wisconsin DHS certified lead renovator, we ensure your project complies with all regulations while protecting students and staff from lead exposure.

    Our lead-safe work practices include proper containment, specialized cleaning, and documentationgiving you peace of mind and regulatory compliance."

    Who's Painting Schools Now?¶

    Current Competitors: - Small local painters (often not lead-certified) - General maintenance contractors - Larger commercial firms (for big projects) - In-house maintenance (touch-up only)

    Competitive Advantages¶

    Factor LaBrant Advantage
    Lead Certification Many competitors lack this
    Commercial Scale Can handle large school projects
    Summer Capacity Dedicated crew availability
    Quality Focus Not lowest-bid positioning
    Local Presence Relationship accessibility

    Differentiation Strategy¶

    Position as "School Painting Specialist": - Create school-specific capability statement - Collect school references and photos - Develop school-specific pricing packages - Emphasize regulatory compliance - Build reputation through referrals

    1. Build Target List

    3. Include: Name, address, contact, building age, last painted

    4. Prioritize by: Size, building age, location

    5. Prepare Materials

    6. School-specific capability statement
    7. Lead certification documentation
    8. Sample proposals
    9. Reference list

    Phase 2: Outreach (February-March 2026)¶

    1. Initial Contact Campaign
    2. Week 1-2: Email to all 50 targets
    3. Week 3-4: Phone follow-up

    4. Week 5-6: Schedule facility visits

    5. Facility Assessments

    6. Free assessments for interested schools
    7. Document scope, building age, condition
    8. Identify lead paint concerns
    9. Prepare detailed proposals

    Phase 3: Proposal & Close (March-April 2026)¶

    1. Submit Proposals
    2. Detailed scope and pricing
    3. Lead-safe work plan (if applicable)
    4. Schedule and timeline

    5. References from similar projects

    6. Follow-Up & Negotiate

    7. Weekly check-ins on pending proposals
    8. Address questions promptly
    9. Negotiate scope/phasing if needed

    3. Daily communication with school contact

    9. Ask for referrals to other schools
