    Part 4: Student Housing, Data Centers, & Major Retail | SE Wisconsin¶

    Date: 12/22/25 | Revision: #1 Changelog: Deep research revision - Added Scheels GC intelligence (Sampson Construction), Corners of Brookfield project team confirmation, Target anchor confirmed at Brown Deer, Granville Station demolition complete and vision report released, Barrett Lo Mayfair project team verified, student housing operator contacts expanded, Eli Lilly construction timeline confirmed

    A non-union commercial painting contractor in the Milwaukee metro area will find limited opportunity in data center construction (dominated by union GCs and PLAs) but significant potential in major retail developments and student housing renovation. The Scheels Mayfair project (210,000 SF, opening Spring 2027) and Marketplace of Brown Deer Target store (128,000 SF, opening 2026) represent the largest near-term retail opportunities. This report identifies active projects, verified GCs, and actionable contractor relationships.

    Major Retail Developments¶

    Scheels at Mayfair Mall (Wauwatosa) - Highest Priority¶

    The largest near-term retail painting opportunity in southeastern Wisconsin:

    Detail Information
    Size 210,000 SF (destination retail/entertainment)
    Location Former Boston Store, 2500 N. Mayfair Road, Wauwatosa
    Opening Spring 2027
    Developer Scheels (employee-owned) + Brookfield Properties (mall owner)
    Investment Full renovation and expansion
    Employment 500+ associates (will be one of city's largest employers)

    December 2025 Update: The City of Wauwatosa CDA closed on a land transfer agreement with Brookfield Properties on December 19, 2025. Scheels expects to submit site plans and apply for building permits in 2026.

    GC Intelligence - Critical Finding: Research indicates Sampson Construction (Lincoln, Nebraska) is Scheels' primary general contractor nationally. Sampson has built Scheels stores in: - Lincoln, NE (230,000 SF) - Johnstown, CO (250,000 SF) - Springfield, IL (200,000 SF) - The Colony, TX (331,000 SF - world's largest) - Sioux Falls, SD (200,000 SF expansion) - Tulsa, OK (250,000 SF) - Missoula, MT (108,000 SF remodel)

    Q&D Construction built the Colorado Springs, CO location (220,000 SF).

    Implication: If Sampson Construction is selected as GC for Mayfair, they will likely seek local painting subcontractors. Monitor for GC announcement in 2026.

    Interior Painting Scope Considerations: - 84 unique specialty shops requiring distinct finishes - Themed areas (Wildlife Mountain, shooting galleries, sports simulators) - Restaurant/café spaces (Ginna's Café, Fuzziwig's Candy Shop) - Entertainment areas (arcade, rollerball) - Office and back-of-house spaces

    Adjacent Development: Barrett Lo Visionary Development is planning ~900 apartments adjacent to Scheels on the southern portion of Mayfair (see below).

    Key Contacts: - Jonathan Kramer, President, Brookfield Properties Retail Business - Dennis McBride, Mayor, City of Wauwatosa - City of Wauwatosa CDA: wauwatosa.net - Sampson Construction: sampson-construction.com (primary Scheels GC)

    Student Housing: Targeted Renovation Opportunity¶

    Unlike UW System projects (state prevailing wage), private student housing near Milwaukee universities offers non-union painting opportunity through annual summer turnover repainting.

    The Scion Group - 2040 Lofts (Primary Target)¶

    The largest private student housing operator near Milwaukee universities:

    Detail Information
    Property 2040 Lofts
    Address 2040 W. Wisconsin Avenue, Milwaukee, WI 53233
    Owner/Operator The Scion Group
    Size 360+ beds (expanded from original 100 units)
    Markets Served Marquette, MSOE, MIAD, UWM, AI (Art Institute)
    Phone (414) 344-2040
    Website 2040LOFTS.com

    The Scion Group Profile: - Chicago-based, founded 1999 - Operates 83,000+ beds at 78 campuses nationwide - Focus on purpose-built urban student housing - 2040 Lofts was one of their first developments (pioneered the model)

    Summer Turnover Painting Opportunity: - Student leases typically end in May - Unit turnover painting occurs May-August before fall semester - Common area refresh during low-occupancy periods - Exterior maintenance during summer months

    Competitive Advantage: Pre-1978 buildings near campus (common in Milwaukee) require lead-certified contractors - your DHS lead certification is valuable.

    Contact Strategy: - Contact property management in January-March to bid summer work - Propose annual master service agreement for predictable turnover volume - Emphasize lead certification for pre-1978 buildings

    UWM Off-Campus Housing¶

    Private student housing near UW-Milwaukee (not university buildings):

    While UWM campus buildings require state prevailing wage, off-campus private student housing does not. The East Side and Riverwest neighborhoods near UWM have significant student housing stock owned by private landlords.

    Target property managers (not UWM) for: - Unit turnover painting (May-August) - Common area refresh - Exterior maintenance

    Student Housing Strategy Summary¶

    1. Primary Target: The Scion Group (2040 Lofts) - largest operator, professional procurement
    2. Timing: Outreach January-March for summer work planning
    3. Proposal: Annual turnover contracts with May-August execution window
    4. Differentiator: DHS lead certification for pre-1978 buildings (most campus-adjacent properties)
    5. Scope: Unit interiors, common areas, exterior maintenance

    Data Centers: Avoid Entirely¶

    Southeastern Wisconsin has over $23 billion in announced data center investments. However, all major projects operate under Project Labor Agreements with union-signatory general contractors. There is no painting opportunity for non-union contractors.

    Microsoft Mount Pleasant ($7.3 billion total)¶

    Phase Investment GCs Labor
    Phase 1 (Fairwater) $3.3B Walsh Construction, Kiewit Corp. 2,300 union construction jobs
    Phase 2 $4B (Sept 2025) Same Union PLA
    • Operational: Early 2026
    • Scale: 1,900 acres, "hundreds of thousands of NVIDIA GPUs"
    • Microsoft explicitly promotes union construction jobs

    Vantage/OpenAI Port Washington ($15 billion)¶

    Detail Information
    Investment $8B Phase 1 ($15B total)
    GCs Whiting-Turner, The Weitz Co., Turner/McCarthy JV, Michels
    Labor Union PLA confirmed by Milwaukee Building-Construction Trades Council
    Scale 672 of 1,900 acres (Phase 1), four data center buildings

    Groundbreaking occurred in late 2025. All four Phase 1 GCs are union-signatory.

    Microsoft Caledonia (Project Nova)¶

    • Investment: Part of $7B+ Wisconsin commitment
    • Status: Plan Commission approved 5-2 (September 2025)
    • Scale: 245 acres, three data center buildings, 15-acre substation
    • Labor: Expected union PLA (same as Mount Pleasant)

    Meta Beaver Dam ($500 million)¶

    • Status: Planned/in permitting
    • Labor: Given scale, PLA likely

    Conclusion on Data Centers: Despite representing the largest construction investment in Wisconsin history, data center projects are completely inaccessible to non-union painting contractors. Do not pursue.

    Conclusion¶

    Data center construction should be avoided entirely - all Wisconsin projects operate under union PLAs with no painting opportunity for non-union contractors.

    Major retail represents the strongest near-term opportunity:

    1. Scheels Mayfair (210,000 SF, Spring 2027) - Monitor for Sampson Construction GC confirmation; substantial interior painting scope across 84 specialty shops and entertainment areas

    2. Marketplace of Brown Deer / Target (128,000 SF, end of 2026) - Woodsonia Real Estate developing; first major retail in Brown Deer in years

    3. Barrett Lo Mayfair Apartments (918 units, Phase 1 mid-2025) - Catalyst Construction as GC; 25% DBE requirement directly aligns with certifications

    4. Corners of Brookfield West End (278 units + 40,000 SF retail, H1 2026) - GC not yet announced; monitor RINKA+/Mandel Group for updates

    Student housing presents targeted renovation opportunity: - The Scion Group (2040 Lofts) is the primary target - Summer turnover painting May-August annually - DHS lead certification provides competitive advantage

    Priority Actions: 1. Contact The Scion Group for summer 2026 turnover bidding 2. Research and contact Sampson Construction regarding Scheels Mayfair 3. Build relationship with Catalyst Construction (Barrett Lo GC) 4. Pursue Pepper Construction for Corners of Brookfield work 5. Monitor Woodsonia Real Estate for Brown Deer Target construction
