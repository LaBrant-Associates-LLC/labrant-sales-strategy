    WisDOT DBE Program: Critical October 2025 Update¶

    Federal Rule Changes Affecting DBE Certification¶

    As of October 3, 2025, the U.S. DOT issued an interim final rule that fundamentally changed the Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE) program:

    Key Changes: - Removed race and sex-based presumptions of social and economic disadvantage - All 1,296 Wisconsin DBE-certified firms must recertify under new criteria - Certification now requires individual demonstration of both social and economic disadvantage

    WisDOT Response: - Temporarily halted setting DBE goals for new federally funded contracts - Stopped counting DBE participation toward existing goals until recertification completes - Developing new recertification processes and guidance - Contracts awarded before October 3, 2025 maintain their original DBE goals

    No federal timeline exists for completing the recertification process. This creates significant uncertainty for transportation painting contractors.

    Impact on Your DBE Certification: - Your WisDOT DBE certification remains valid but requires recertification under new criteria - Contact WisDOT Civil Rights office at osecdbe@dot.wi.gov for recertification status and timeline - The certification still automatically qualifies you as TBE for Milwaukee County projects (unaffected by federal pause) - City of Milwaukee SBE certification operates independently and remains fully active with 10% bid preference

    Wisconsin Competitive Bidding Thresholds¶

    Entity Type Threshold Statute Requirement
    State contracts $50,000 Sec. 16.855 Formal sealed bids
    County contracts $25,000 Sec. 59.52(29) Formal sealed bids
    City contracts $25,000 Sec. 62.15 Formal sealed bids
    Village contracts $25,000 Sec. 61.54 Formal sealed bids
    Town contracts $25,000 Sec. 60.47 Formal sealed bids

    $5,000-$25,000: Class I public notice required before contract execution

    Under $5,000: Best judgment purchasing applies

    Water Tower Painting Note: Wisconsin DWD advises that combined contracts to renovate, paint, and maintain municipally-owned water towers are subject to prevailing wage law (sec. 66.0903(3)(am)) if the project exceeds thresholds. Municipalities must apply to DWD for prevailing wage rate determination.
